Websites you can use to learn:


Here are some free and paid resources you can use to continue to expand your programming knowledge.


Freecodecamp:


"Learn to code and help nonprofits."


A free elearning platform for all ages.

Freecodecamp has numerous courses that make up their cirriculum covering everything from web development to data science. These courses are made of detailed walkthroughs and thousands of exercises that will help users learn the basics of the language and apply these skills on multiple projects for their portfolio. At the end of each course, users get free certifications that they can put on their resume.


KhanAcademy (Check out their Computing section in courses):


"A Free World-Class Education for Anyone, Anywhere"


A personalized learning resource for all ages

Khan Academy offers practice exercises, instructional videos, and a personalized learning dashboard that empower learners to study programming at their own pace. They also offer hunderds of practice quizzes and exercises to help users solidfy their programming skills.


W3Schools (You can learn many languages):


"The largest web developer site on the internet. 3 billion pages displayed each year. 60 million visitors each month."


Creates simplified and interactive learning experiences

W3Schools has focus on simplicity and easy learning. It breaks down programming lanaguages and offers detailed walkthroughs and examples for each section. It also offers practices exercises and quizzes after each section so users can practice what they learned. Furthermore, users have the opportunity to learn about advanced topics like other modules in the language and how they are used to create real-world applications.



GeeksforGeeks:


"Being the Pro in Programming can be easy, especially if you start with us!"


Prepare With Us, Get Hired With Us, and Grow With Us

GeeksforGeeks provides a variety of services for you to learn computer science and also have fun! It offers free tutorials, thousands of programming articles, online courses, coding competitions, webinars by industry experts, and job opportunities for programmers and other individuals in the tech industry.


Feel free to sign up and take courses with these websites:


Here are some additional resources you can use. You may need to pay for some of the courses and certifications offered on these websites.


Coursera (Check out their Computer Science section in courses):


"Take the World's Best Courses, Online, For Free"


Coursera offers thousands of paid and free courses on computer science, AI, machine learning, game development, data science, and other topics for new programmers. It also offers certifications for a small fee that will help you boost your resume and attract employers.



Udemy:


"Changing learning for the better"


Udemy has a curated collection of technical courses on computer science topics, similiar to Coursera. These courses have video tutorials, quizzes, project resources, and exercises for students to complete. Furthermore, Udemy has many sales throughout the year where courses can be bought at a price as low as $12 or $13, so its a very affordable option.
